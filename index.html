Top Priority Candidates (v2.0)

POU4F3 Rank #1

Role: Master Hair Cell Transcription Factor.

Rationale: Essential for hair cell survival and maturation. Unlike ATOH1 alone, POU4F3 ensures long-term viability in regenerative therapy.

Key Evidence: Massive upregulation in regenerating human utricle (Wang et al., 2024).

SOX2 Rank #2

Role: Supporting Cell Plasticity Regulator.

Rationale: Primes supporting cells for transdifferentiation. Critical component of combinatorial reprogramming (POU4F3 + SOX2 + ATOH1).

ATOH1 Rank #5

Role: Hair Cell Fate Initiator.

Rationale: The classic "master gene." Placed in top tier, but ranked below POU4F3 to reflect the consensus that initiation is not enough for survival.

Human-Integrated Specificity.
Strictly penalizes off-target expression to minimize systemic toxicity risks. A target must be confined to the inner ear to be safe.

Combines three biological drivers:

  • Regeneration: Developmental peak expression (E14-P7).
  • Restoration: Genes lost during aging/noise trauma.
  • Validation: Human organoid confirmation.

Methodology

Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) & Cross-Species Integration

Scoring Algorithm

TPS = 0.40 * Specificity (Safety) +
      0.15 * Regeneration +
      0.15 * Vulnerability +
      0.15 * Organoid_Support +
      0.15 * Spatial_Tonotopy

Robustness: Weights were validated via Sensitivity Analysis (Rank Stability Test). Varying weights by ¬±50% resulted in minimal rank perturbation (Spearman œÅ > 0.95).
