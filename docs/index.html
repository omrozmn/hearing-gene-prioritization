Therapeutic Priority Score (TPS)

Multi-Module Framework for Inner Ear Therapeutic Target Prioritization

What is TPS?

TPS (Therapeutic Priority Score) is a computational framework derived from 41,697 human inner-ear cells that systematically prioritizes therapeutic gene targets for hearing loss and inner ear regeneration.

By integrating hair cell specificity, developmental programs, aging vulnerability, and genetic associations, TPS identifies the most promising candidates for experimental validation and drug development.

Why It Matters

TPS is the first multi-atlas, multi-module framework specifically designed for human inner ear therapeutics. Unlike single-dataset approaches, TPS integrates:

  • Real differential expression from 2,297 human hair cells (100% real data, no mock)
  • Developmental trajectories and aging vulnerability
  • GWAS associations and literature curation
  • Cross-species conservation (optional)

Impact: Accelerates research by providing data-driven priorities for gene therapy, drug development, and regenerative medicine strategies.

Pipeline Overview

TPS integrates 9 datasets across 5 modules to compute a composite priority score for each gene.

1. Data Sources (100% Real, No Mock)

Dataset Species Cells Purpose
GSE213796 Human 41,697 HC Specificity (2,297 HC cells)
GSE114157 Mouse 3,000 SGN Specificity
GSE60019 Mouse 16 samples Developmental Peak
GSE274279 Mouse 3,000 Aging Decline
Literature Human - GWAS Deafness Genes

2. Quality Control

  • Min genes per cell: 200
  • Max mitochondrial %: 20%
  • Normalization: log1p(CPM)
  • Downsampling: REMOVED (full 41K cells used)

3. Module Scoring

Module Method Weight
HC Specificity Differential Expression (Scanpy rank_genes_groups) 30%
SGN Specificity Differential Expression 20%
Dev Peak Max expression across developmental stages 20%
Aging Decline Mature - Aging expression difference 15%
GWAS Binary (known deafness gene) 15%
Conservation DISABLED (weight=0) 0%

4. TPS Formula

TPS = 0.30 √ó HC_specificity 
    + 0.20 √ó SGN_specificity
    + 0.20 √ó Dev_peak
    + 0.15 √ó Aging_decline
    + 0.00 √ó Conservation (disabled)
    + 0.15 √ó GWAS

5. Validation Tests

  • Enrichment Analysis: Hypergeometric test against known gene sets
  • LOCO (Leave-One-Component-Out): Stability testing
  • Negative Control: Randomized score distribution
  • Literature Validation: Top genes vs. PubMed citations

Key Methodological Decisions

Why NO Downsampling?

Initial pipeline downsampled to 3,000 cells, resulting in only 214 HC cells. This caused:

  • Negative mean HC marker expression
  • Failed differential expression (all genes ‚Üí constant 0.0837)

Solution: Removed downsampling ‚Üí 2,297 HC cells ‚Üí successful DE

Why Conservation Disabled?

Cross-species conservation initially crashed (all genes ‚Üí 0.0000) due to:

  • Incomplete ortholog mapping
  • Species-specific expression differences

Solution: Set weight=0, focus on robust HC specificity signal
